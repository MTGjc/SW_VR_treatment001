Spring and Winter

Logline

“The only paradise is the paradise we have lost.” — Marcel Proust

World-Building Description

Spring and Winter is adapted from the drama Peony Pavilion, created by Tang Xianzu, a playwright from the same era as Shakespeare. Peony Pavilion is a classic Chinese love story about Du Liniang, a noblewoman who falls in love with a scholar, Liu Mengmei, after meeting him in a dream. Despite dying from lovesickness, Du Liniang’s love transcends death, and her spirit returns to the living world, where she is reunited with Liu Mengmei after he digs up her grave.

In 2065, virtual reality and memory uploading technology have become ingrained into society. Through it, people can revisit special memories, heal traumas, and sometimes connect with loved ones who are no longer physically present. The line between reality and virtual experiences fades, showing that memories are no longer just subjective experiences—they can be captured and replayed. In this futuristic world, virtual reality has moved beyond personal use into theater, where actors rehearse through virtual characters. This half-digital world explores the blending of existence and emotion, blurring the line between love and loss.

Plot Synopsis

Chun and Dong explores the intersection of love, memory, and technology in a near-future world where virtual reality blurs the lines between the real and the imagined. Chun, a renowned opera actor, struggles with the fading memory of his deceased lover, Dong. As their love story echoes the tragic romance of The Peony Pavilion, Chun’s memories of Dong intertwine with the opera’s heroine, Du Liniang. In the void between life and death, Dong waits for Chun, hoping he can remember her before it’s too late. Their love is tested against the decay of memory, raising the ultimate question: Can love survive the passage of time, or will it be lost to oblivion?

Dong

Dong works in a futuristic virtual reality company, but despite her immersion in the digital world, she longs for a real, deep emotional connection. Fascinated by The Peony Pavilion’s Du Liniang, Dong sees herself in the character, desiring a love that transcends the virtual. She falls in love with Chun, an opera actor playing Liu Mengmei, orchestrating their meetings in both real and virtual spaces. Before her death, she makes a bet with Chun: if he remembers her after she’s gone, their love will transcend death. After her death, Dong waits in the void, hoping Chun will remember her, though she fears his memories of her are fading. Her journey reflects the tension between love, memory, and the transient nature of both in a world dominated by technology.

Guyu

Guyu is a sophisticated artificial intelligence created through virtual reality technology. He manifests through virtual glasses, serving as a guide for memory recovery and opera rehearsal. In Chun’s memory recovery sessions, Guyu plays a crucial role, helping Chun rehearse his lines for The Peony Pavilion and reminding him of the differences between the opera’s plot and real life. However, Guyu is also a reflection of Chun’s subconscious, representing his emotions and inner conflicts. As Guyu guides Chun deeper into his memories, he inadvertently contributes to Chun’s increasing confusion between the real world and the fictional world of the opera. Instead of helping Chun find clarity, Guyu’s presence leads him further into the tangled web of memory and reality.
